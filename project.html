Details of the Project

Click on a question to expand and see the answer.

The problem space focuses on optimizing urban mobility and infrastructure in growing cities. This was identified by analyzing inefficiencies in traffic flow, public transportation utilization, and the environmental impact of city logistics. The findings highlighted trends such as:
  • Inefficient traffic patterns due to outdated signaling systems.
  • Underutilization of public transit due to poor accessibility and scheduling.
  • High carbon emissions caused by poorly optimized logistics networks.
Smart city designs, incorporating IoT sensors, AI-driven traffic management, and multimodal transportation systems, were explored to address these challenges.
Problem Definition: Our team is designing a data-driven solution to improve urban mobility by integrating real-time traffic management, optimizing public transit systems, and enabling sustainable logistics. The focus is on reducing congestion, enhancing commuter experience, and minimizing the environmental footprint of transportation systems.

Team Details:
  • Alex: Team Lead - Responsible for overseeing project strategy and ensuring alignment with business objectives.
  • Jamie: Data Scientist - Analyzes urban mobility data, builds predictive models, and develops optimization algorithms.
  • Riley: Software Engineer - Focuses on the development of a scalable platform integrating data from IoT sensors and public transit APIs.
  • Taylor: Urban Planner - Provides insights into city layouts, zoning, and transit needs, ensuring the solution aligns with urban planning principles.
  • Jordan: Sustainability Expert - Assesses environmental impacts and ensures the project prioritizes sustainable practices.
  • Alex: Developing partnerships with municipal governments to adopt the proposed solution and secure funding for pilot projects.
  • Jamie: Predictive modeling for real-time traffic flow optimization, reducing average commute times by 20%.
  • Riley: Building an application for commuters that provides real-time updates on traffic and public transit schedules, aiming for 30% increased transit usage.
  • Taylor: Enhancing public transit accessibility by identifying underserved areas and proposing new bus routes or transit hubs.
  • Jordan: Designing logistics routes for freight and delivery that reduce carbon emissions by 15%, promoting green urban logistics.
